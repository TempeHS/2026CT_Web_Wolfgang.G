Carcinization

Carcinization is a term for an evolutionary phenomenon of multiple different clades evolving into crabs, it is a wonderful example of convergent evolution


Origins of the word

The phrase was coined in 1916 by British zoologist L.A Borradaile

who described it as "one of the many attempts in nature to evolve a crab". Essentially, evolution seems to have a weird habit of turning non-crab-like crustaceans into crabs.


It's still debated why this happens so often, but it's thought that the Crab Shape (broad, flattened, body, strong claws, trucked tail)
